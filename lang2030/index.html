Scripts for intro and findings (actual presentation may be slightly different)

Good morning everyone. Today we Group 1 are going to discuss about Ethiopian Airlines Boeing 737 Max crash. (Group introduction)

At the beginning want to make a brief introduction of the crash.

On 10th March 2019, Ethiopian Airlines Flight 302 took off from Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. 6 minutes after takeoff, the plane crashed at a nearby town, killing all of 157 people aboard. The jet was 737 Max and was first introduced in 2017. Due to this crash as well as a former one, Lion Air Flight 610, 737 max now has been grounded all over the world.

Next, I am going to share with you some of my findings regarded to the cause of the crash.

First I am going to talk about the direct cause - the new computerized system on 737 Max. Why it is installed and how is it related to the crash? In order to increase the efficiency of engine, Boeing engineers used a larger and heavier engine for Max. And to compensate the change in the center of gravity, a new system called the Maneuvering Characteristics Augmentation System, or we call it the MCAS, was implemented to Max. Its function is to modify the angle between the airplane and the horizontal line, or the Angle of Attack. Once the sensor tells the angle is too small, then the MCAS will be activated automatically to adjust the angle. Boeing software engineers authorized the MCAS with highest priority, pilots can never stop it. In this case, the sensor output wrong number and the plane lost its control and crashed. But, an experienced pilot said that the sensor may have wrong output all the time during the flight due to the environmental changes or vibration. This potential bug seems to be overlooked by Boeing engineers.

The Second finding is that Boeing actually did not provide enough training and instructions about the MCAS. The MCAS is unique to Max, so pilots knew nothing about it before they operate the plane. Pilots at United Airlines said that they were provided with a 13-page guide to 737 Max. The guide did not mention a word about the MCAS. Essential information about the MCAS was only sold as options. By doing so, Boeing saved millions of dollar in developing simulators and conducting training for pilots.

The last finding is about the flaws in regulations. Federal Aviation Administration or FAA is in charge for legislation and revision of aviation law. The former FAA engineer revealed that, the revision of regulations was rushed to reach a certain date. This kind of hurried and careless process leads to flaws in regulations. 737 was put into use 70 years ago. Although great revolutions in technology have taken place, the rules that should be followed have not changed very much over more than seventy years. It is even more astonishing to see that almost change in regulation has been made considering over 500 crashes occurred in the past 70 years, killing more than 50,000 people. To conclude for the third point, the regulations are outdated, for FAA pays few attention to the technology development and the fatal crashes.

Then I will pass to Louis to show us the ethical implications.
